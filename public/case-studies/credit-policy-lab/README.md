# Credit Policy Lab

Status: greenfield deterministic policy/backtest vertical slice built in 2026-07. The inherited
Streamlit scoring demo remains prior work only and is not required for this experience.

## Decision

A fictional credit policy manager sets two thresholds and review capacity. Synthetic calibrated
PD feeds expected loss, then a separate policy layer produces approve, review, or decline bands.
The model score is never presented as the final business decision.

## Data and license

`synthetic-credit-data.json` and CSV are generated by `npm run generate:analytics` with fixed seed
`2026071302`. It contains 12,000 fictional applications across 12 synthetic vintages, with explicit
train/calibration/backtest splits, no PII, no external credit records, and no recovered
production-model output. Application, booked loan, generated observed outcome, score, and policy
decision remain separate entities or stages. The original synthetic fixture
is covered by the portfolio `NOTICE.md`.

The same 12,000 rows are available as JSON, CSV, and real ZSTD-compressed Parquet. The Parquet
derivative is generated and row-count checked with `npm run generate:analytics-parquet` using the
locked DuckDB-Wasm dependency.

## Method and validation

- Raw and calibrated PD are deterministic synthetic scores used to exercise policy mechanics.
- Expected loss is `PD x LGD x EAD`.
- Approve, manual-review, and decline bands are mutually exclusive and exhaustive.
- Queue overflow is explicit when review volume exceeds analyst capacity.
- The browser recomputes synthetic Brier scores, baseline/challenger comparisons, vintage backtests, PSI, and descriptive approval
  slices. These are newly computed fixture results, not model-performance claims on real data.
- Reason codes are deterministic mappings from fictional input fields.

## Governance and monitoring

The UI separates score, economics, policy, human review, and audit. A production version would
monitor calibration, score/feature drift, queue SLA, override rate, loss vintage, and descriptive
group slices; it would require legal/compliance review before any fairness or regulatory claim.

## Failure conditions and limitations

Invalid contracts or thresholds block policy publication. This slice does not claim regulatory
compliance, real-world fairness, real applicant outcomes, deployed model accuracy, or causal impact.
ONNX scoring remains a future option and is not required for the stable static demo.
