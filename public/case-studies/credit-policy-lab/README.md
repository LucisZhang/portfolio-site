# Credit Policy Lab

Status: dual-source, deterministic policy/backtest case study. The committed scored Lending Club
artifact is requested by default. Missing or invalid real bytes fail closed and leave the
separately labeled fixed-seed synthetic fallback active.

## Decision workflow

A credit policy manager compares baseline and challenger PD, applies approval/review thresholds,
computes expected loss from disclosed PD/LGD/EAD inputs, manages review capacity, and records a
policy decision. Model scores, economics, policy, human review, and audit remain separate stages.

## Synthetic fallback

`synthetic-credit-data.json` and CSV are generated by `npm run generate:analytics` with fixed seed
`2026071302`. They contain 12,000 fictional applications across 12 synthetic vintages with explicit
train/calibration/backtest splits, generated outcomes, deterministic scores, no PII, and no
external credit records. The fixture is covered by the portfolio `NOTICE.md`; its metrics are not
real-model validation.

## Committed real artifact

Real mode loads [scored-backtest.parquet](scored-backtest.parquet), a 120,000-row deterministic
sample from the 1,347,681-row UCM-curated Lending Club granting archive. It contains disjoint
72,000 train / 24,000 isotonic-calibration / 24,000 later-backtest rows, observed final outcomes,
offline logistic/XGBoost scores, and three directional SHAP reason codes per application.

Raw source rows, direct IDs, ZIP, title, and description are not committed. Published identifiers
are deterministic pseudonyms: linkable within the artifact and dictionary-linkable by a holder of
the public source, not anonymous. The exact output-schema gate, 95 MiB browser budget, time split,
score bounds, outcome types, and reason-code contract fail closed.

## Modeling and claim boundaries

- Baseline: L2-regularized logistic regression; challenger: depth-4 XGBoost. Both use nine disclosed
  application-time source features and are independently isotonic-calibrated on the calibration
  window. Score columns retain float64 precision; all reported model metrics use the later backtest
  window and are computed after rereading the final Parquet.
- EAD is requested amount. LGD is a disclosed 45% assumption. Legacy browser fields absent from the
  archive use explicit unavailable sentinels and do not enter either model.
- Observed swap-set default rates describe the loaded outcome window; they do not prove challenger
  superiority.
- The archive contains granted loans only. Rejected applicants and acceptance-population policy
  effects are not represented. Home-ownership slices are descriptive and are not fairness evidence.

The generated [backtest report](backtest-report.json) and
[methods evidence](methods-evidence.json) are hash-linked to the committed Parquet. This case study
recomputes AUC, Brier, log-loss, and default rate from those exact serialized columns during
verification. It does not claim causal impact, live or production decisioning, regulatory
validation, deployed accuracy, or real-world fairness.
